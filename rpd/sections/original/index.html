Original data is data without a source; the earliest form of that data.

...but how does data originate? Isn’t all data somehow influenced by data that came before it? Is there really such a thing as an original?

In the following experiment, I analysed an image with the goal of tracing its origin.

So did I succeed in finding the original?

Yes and no. My personal definition of the term “original” changed as I conducted this experiment. Rather than thinking about an original as a singular entity, I started thinking about it as a set of influences all of which culminate in the work being analysed.

Then what is originality?

Throughout the long lineage of influences, we find that some works build upon their predecessors more than others (via remix, AKA (spoiler alert) “Manipulation”). As such, I believe originality to be a spectrum, which ranges from what the populous intuitively considers a “copy” or a duplicate to what is typically considered an original.
