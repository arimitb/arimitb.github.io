You just changed the contents of a file, making it different from how it used to be.

How does it feel to be this powerful?

To manipulate data is to change it in some manner such that it is distinguished from its previous state.

...but can we really “change” data? Or does attempting to do so only result in new data?

In the following workshop, I collected and printed 4 images from a database and tried to manipulate them through the method of collage. The goal was to change the meaning of the images in order to craft a new story.


Original images:

As I cropped, masked, and transformed the images by cutting and pasting, I tried to re-use certain leftover elements in other collages as I saw fit. Having only one copy of each image was an interesting restriction in that regard.

So did the images lose their original meaning?

I believe the meaning of the images was “manipulated” to a certain extent. More than the visual properties of the images, it is stacking the contexts surrounding them which lend their meaning to the resulting collages.

If someone encounters these collages without having seen their sources, would they form a different interpretation of these images?

Looking at the collages in a vacuum (whatever that might mean in a practical context), the collages would only retain the meaning that they carry via their visual properties. Stripping away the contextual meaning, we are left with a sliver of the substance that they previously had.
